Executive Summary

This Capstone represents a comprehensive HIPAA project completed at the end of my graduate program. The primary goal was to become familiar with HIPAA requirements and to create a framework for conducting an audit. It provides both a risk assessment and an audit plan tailored for the unique risks and compliance needs of deploying an on‑premises large language model in a healthcare environment.

  • Goal: build familiarity with HIPAA safeguards and design an audit framework.
  • Scope: administrative, physical, and technical safeguards mapped to concrete LLM controls.
  • Method: control statements, test procedures, and evidence (configs, logs, and operational artifacts).
  • Data Protection: de‑identification for model context; PHI governed by EHR RBAC/MFA; encryption in transit & at rest.
  • Artifacts: SRA export, HIPAA standards deck, LLM audit draft, BAA, and an LLM‑specific risks assessment.

What the HHS SRA Tool Provides

The HHS Security Risk Assessment (SRA) tool generates a structured, educational risk review for HIPAA: it captures org context, asks section‑by‑section questions, shows best‑practice notes, and rolls up risk ratings by domain. The export becomes evidence for policy maturity, training, access control, encryption, and more—useful baseline context before layering LLM‑specific risks.

The SRA is informative and comprehensive, but it’s not a guarantee of compliance; it’s an aid that frames threats, controls, and documentation you must maintain and verify.

LLM Audit – Project Draft (Why/What)

The draft defines a HIPAA compliance audit for the DeepSeek R1 deployment at EVMC: purpose, scope, audience, and evidence‑gathering steps. It positions the work in the Third Line of Defence—independent from build/operate teams—and focuses on verifying safeguards relevant to AI systems (access control, logging, de‑identification workflow, and residual risk). It also clarifies that this is a compliance audit plan, not a replacement for risk analysis or risk management.
